I record my sleep every night and whenever I work out. For the purpose of this graph, I took the past 30 days of my sleep data from my Apple Watch and a binary "Yes" or "No" if I worked out.

This plot shows the hours of sleep I had every night, with green indicating if I worked out that day and red indicating if I did not work out that day.

Grad school is definitely taking a toll on me given the amount of sleep that I'm getting and how it seems to be related to my workout days.

In order to account for more interactive features in the chart specific to D3, I switched from chart.min.js to /d3-legend.min.js

I added two features to this graph from the previous version in Homework 1: A highlight feature and hover-over mouse feature. The hover-over mouse function provides the full data for each data point in a ‘tool tip’ format. This feature allows you to drill into the specific data and see the full data that went into that bar.

The highlight feature allows you to click an entry that you are hovering over to highlight all common days of that week to see a common height amongst a specific weekday. This feature allows you to see common trends by a specific day of the week. It may also be insightful to know that you do not have any trend on specific days..
